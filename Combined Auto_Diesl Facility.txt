## Combination Automotive / Diesel Mechanics Facility (Recommended)

A **combination facility** is designed to support **both light-duty automotive and medium/heavy-duty diesel instruction** within a single, integrated space. This approach maximizes flexibility, funding opportunities, and workforce relevance.

---

### Why a Combination Facility?

**1. Workforce Alignment**

* Many employers service both automotive and diesel platforms
* Students gain broader, more marketable skills
* Supports fleet, agricultural, municipal, and dealership pathways

**2. Efficient Use of Space**

* Shared classrooms, tool rooms, and support spaces
* Separate but connected lab zones for automotive and diesel
* Reduces duplication of expensive infrastructure

**3. Funding & Industry Support**

* Attractive to a wider range of industry partners
* Eligible for more grant opportunities
* Easier to justify capital investment to boards

---

### Recommended Facility Zones

#### Light-Duty Automotive Lab

* 8‚Äì12 service bays
* Two-post lifts
* Electrical & diagnostics stations
* Alignment rack

#### Diesel / Heavy-Duty Lab

* 4‚Äì6 oversized bays
* In-ground or four-post lifts
* Overhead cranes or hoists
* High-capacity ventilation

#### Shared Spaces

* Classroom(s) with direct lab access
* Tool room & parts storage
* Welding & fabrication area
* Engine / drivetrain lab
* Instructor offices
* Student lockers & restrooms

* **Clear separation of vehicle flow** (auto vs. diesel)
* **Increased ceiling height** in diesel bays (20‚Äì25 ft typical)
* **Reinforced floor slabs** for heavy vehicles
* **Noise and exhaust control** between lab zones
* **Full instructor visibility** across all bays
